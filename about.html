About Me

Professional Background

I am a Mechanical Engineer based in Adelaide, South Australia, with a passion for engineering design, fluid mechanics, and hydropower systems. I hold a Bachelor of Engineering (Mechanical Engineering — Hydropower Specialisation) from Kathmandu University, Nepal, and a Master of Engineering (Mechanical Engineering) from Engineering Institute of Technology, Melbourne.

I have been formally assessed by Engineers Australia as a Professional Mechanical Engineer (ANZSCO 233512, EA ID: 10193715), confirming my competency to practice as a mechanical engineer in Australia.

Engineering Experience

My engineering journey began in Nepal where I worked on the Mai Beni Hydropower Project (9.51 MW), supervising penstock fabrication, conducting non-destructive testing, and managing hydrostatic pressure testing. This hands-on experience gave me deep insights into real-world engineering challenges and project management.

I then worked as a Mechanical Engineer at Kathmandu Hydro Electric, where I designed and modified hydromechanical components using AutoCAD and SolidWorks, and implemented systems to improve inspection efficiency by 30%.

In Australia, I completed my Master's degree while developing strong leadership and safety management skills. I led teams of 6–10 workers for over 3 years, maintaining a zero Lost Time Injury record and improving operational efficiency by 20%.

Why Mechanical Engineering?

I am fascinated by how mechanical systems work — from turbines in hydropower plants to the optimization of designs through CFD analysis. I enjoy solving complex problems and working with teams to deliver high-quality engineering solutions.

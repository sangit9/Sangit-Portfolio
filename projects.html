Design Optimization of Dimple Surface on NACA 0012 Airfoil

Master's Capstone Project | February – May 2024

Engineering Institute of Technology, Melbourne

Project Overview

Investigated how surface modifications (dimples) on an airfoil affect aerodynamic performance using computational fluid dynamics. The goal was to optimize the dimple geometry to improve lift and drag characteristics.

Methodology

  • Literature Review: Conducted comprehensive research on NACA airfoils and aerodynamic effects of surface modifications.
  • 3D Modelling: Developed a 3D NACA 0012 airfoil model in SolidWorks with various dimple configurations.
  • CFD Simulations: Performed detailed computational fluid dynamics simulations using ANSYS Fluent for baseline and modified configurations.
  • Optimization: Systematically optimized dimple geometry parameters (diameter: 1.375 mm, spacing: 9.9 mm, depth: 0.55 mm).
  • Analysis: Analyzed aerodynamic performance metrics including lift coefficient (Cl) and drag coefficient (Cd).

Results

Successfully improved aerodynamic performance through design optimization, achieving:

  • Lift Coefficient: Cl = 0.8206
  • Drag Coefficient: Cd = 0.0515
  • Measurable improvements in lift-to-drag ratio
  • Validated CFD methodology against theoretical predictions

  • Design optimization methodology
  • Technical writing and presentation
  • Research methodology and data analysis

Technologies & Tools

Numerical Investigation of Pump as Turbine (PAT) Performance

Bachelor's Capstone Project | November 2019 – October 2020

Kathmandu University, Nepal

Project Overview

Investigated the feasibility of using standard centrifugal pumps as turbines in micro-hydropower applications. This research aimed to provide a cost-effective solution for hydropower generation in remote areas of Nepal where conventional turbines are expensive.

Research Objective

Determine if a standard pump can operate efficiently in reverse mode (as a turbine) under various flow conditions, and quantify the efficiency improvements.

Methodology

  • Literature Review: Reviewed existing PAT research and turbine technologies relevant to micro-hydropower in Nepal.
  • Scenario Design: Designed experimental scenarios to evaluate pump performance under turbine operating conditions.
  • CFD Simulations: Performed numerical simulations to model flow through the pump operating in reverse mode.
  • Comparative Analysis: Analyzed performance in clean flow and sediment-laden flow environments (common in Nepali rivers).

Key Results

  • Demonstrated 40% efficiency improvement when pump operates in turbine mode under optimal conditions
  • Provided performance curves for various flow conditions
  • Analyzed effects of sediment on system performance
  • Concluded PAT is viable for micro-hydropower applications in Nepal

Skills Demonstrated

  • Fluid mechanics analysis
  • Research methodology
  • Academic writing
  • Problem-solving for real-world engineering challenges
